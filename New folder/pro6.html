The Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza is an ancient Mayan city located in the Yucatan Peninsula of Mexico. The site is considered one of the most important archaeological sites in Mexico and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city was once a major center of Mayan civilization and is now one of the most popular tourist destinations in Mexico.

One of the most famous structures at Chichen Itza is the Kukulcan Pyramid, also known as El Castillo. The pyramid is a 98-foot tall structure with four sides, each containing 91 steps, and a temple at the top.
