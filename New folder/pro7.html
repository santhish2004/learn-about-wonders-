Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. The site is one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world and is considered a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city was built in the 15th century and was abandoned during the Spanish conquest, remaining hidden for centuries until it was rediscovered in 1911 by Hiram Bingham, an American explorer.

Machu Picchu is not only an impressive archaeological site but also an important cultural and historical landmark. The site provides insight into the lives and beliefs of the Inca people, and their remarkable engineering and architectural achievements. It is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, archaeology, or culture, and a testament to the enduring legacy of one of the world's most fascinating civilizations.
